<Section icon={Map} title="Optimized Leveling and Farming Routes">

Navigating the world of Jujutsu Legacy requires knowing exactly where to go at each level interval. Wandering into the Culling Games at Level 100 will result in a quick death, while staying at Jujutsu High past Level 150 will significantly slow down your progression toward the **jujutsu legacy max level**.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Starter Loop (Level 1-125)">
    Begin your journey at Jujutsu High. Focus on completing the starter quests and defeating early curse enemies. Once you hit Level 5, open the menu (M key) and enter all active codes to get your first batch of Technique and Clan Spins. Aim for a technique that provides decent area-of-effect (AoE) damage to speed up early mob clearing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Shibuya & Metro Expansion (Level 125-450)">
    Move toward the Shibuya bridge and the Metro. This area introduces significant boss checkpoints. You will encounter Choso, Mahito, and Jogo. These bosses are essential for testing your build's durability. If you find yourself dying too often, spend your stat points on Health and Cursed Energy before pushing further.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Culling Games Entry (Level 500-700)">
    This is where the game truly opens up. The Culling Games area is home to JudgeMan, Naoya, and Higuruma. Farming JudgeMan is a high priority here, as he drops the Gavel Hammer and Executioner Sword. This phase is also critical for players looking to unlock the Uro or Ishigori vessels.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Shinjuku Grind (Level 1000-2000)">
    The final stretch to the max level takes place in Shinjuku and the Raid zones. You will face Kashimo and Sukuna-related bosses. At this stage, raw levels matter less than your equipment. You must farm the **Strongest Raid** and **Divine General Raid** to obtain endgame titles like "Honored One" and traits like "Six Eyes."
  </Step>
</Steps>

Always prioritize quest chains that are geographically close to each other. For example, the Takuma Ino route at Jujutsu High is much faster for repeatable EXP than hunting isolated mobs across the map.
</Callout>
