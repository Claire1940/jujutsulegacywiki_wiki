<Section icon={Map} title="Understanding Boss Mechanics in Jujutsu Legacy">

Defeating the various **jujutsu legacy bosses** is the primary method to obtain rare items, weapons, and cursed techniques. In this game, bosses are divided into two distinct categories: World Bosses and Quest Bosses. Understanding how these categories operate is essential for planning your farming routes and maximizing your character's progression.

World Bosses are non-player characters scattered across the map that do not require any active quests to yield rewards. They can be engaged freely by any player in the server, making them highly contested targets. These bosses drop a wide variety of items, including Swords, Vessels, Fighting Styles, Curse Techniques, Accessories, Traits, and Races.

Quest Bosses are tied to specific missions. To receive their rewards, you must have the corresponding quest active in your log before initiating the fight. If a Quest Boss does not have any unique drops listed in the database, their rewards are distributed exclusively through the completion of their associated quest.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Jujutsu Legacy Bosses in Jujutsu High and Shibuya">

The early to mid-game progression takes place primarily across Jujutsu High and the Shibuya/Metro districts. These areas host several key encounters that provide foundational techniques and rare titles.

In Jujutsu High, the first NPC you will encounter is the Damage Tester. Located at the bottom right of the spawn area, this NPC is completely invincible and cannot die. While it does not yield any drops or experience, it serves as a critical tool for calculating your damage output, testing combo chains, and evaluating the scaling of new cursed techniques.

Near the bridge leading to Shibuya, you will find Megumi. This boss is relatively straightforward but requires careful positioning to avoid being knocked off the bridge. Megumi drops the Dark Surge technique, which is a highly sought-after ability for mid-game builds.

Moving into the Shibuya and Metro regions, the difficulty increases significantly. Mechamaru spawns directly next to the red and white towers near the Metro entrance. Defeating Mechamaru gives you a chance to obtain the Puppet Manipulation technique, which excels in long-range zoning. 

Toji is located to the right of the entrance to Shinjuku, next to a tall white building. Toji is a high-speed physical fighter who drops the Heavenly Restriction trait. Due to the low drop rate, preparing a high-damage build is recommended to speed up the farming process.

Inside the Metro area, you will encounter Choso, a Special Grade Quest Boss. Choso is positioned across from Mahito near the Metro spawn point. Defeating Choso yields the Blood Master Title, but only under a strict condition: you must land the final blow while using the Blood Manipulation Technique.

| Boss Name | Area Location | Key Drop Item | Drop Chance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Damage Tester** | Jujutsu High Spawn | None (Invincible) | 0% |
| **Megumi** | Jujutsu High Bridge | Dark Surge | **10%** |
| **Mechamaru** | Metro Entrance | Puppet Manipulation | **4%** |
| **Toji** | Shinjuku Entrance | Heavenly Restriction | **1%** |
| **Choso** | Metro Spawn Area | Blood Master Title | **100% (Conditional)** |

<Callout type="tip" title="Choso Farming Tip">
When farming Choso for the Blood Master Title, coordinate with a party to lower his health, but ensure the player with Blood Manipulation lands the concluding blow.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Culling Games and Shinjuku Boss Encounters">

The late-game content in Jujutsu Legacy shifts focus to the Culling Games and the ruins of Shinjuku. The bosses in these zones feature complex attack patterns, higher health pools, and powerful weapon drops.

In the Culling Games zone, Todo spawns near the Divine General Raid. Todo utilizes fast-paced physical strikes and teleportation-style maneuvers. Defeating him gives a small chance to obtain the Vibra Slap, an accessory that boosts utility and attack speed. Nearby, the Special Grade Quest Boss JudgeMan spawns on the right side of the road near the Culling Games spawn point. JudgeMan drops the Gavel Hammer, a heavy melee weapon with excellent crowd-control capabilities.

In Shinjuku, players must prepare for high-tier encounters. Yuji Shinjuku spawns next to Heian Sukuna. This fight demands constant movement to avoid his close-range physical combos. Yuji drops the Chosen Vessel item, which is essential for advanced character progression.

Deep within Shinjuku, Kashimo spawns on a stone platform located in the middle of an open area off the main road. To find him, take the first right turn off the straight road. Kashimo utilizes lightning-based attacks that can stun players. Defeating him yields the Electric Pole, a powerful polearm weapon.

| Boss Name | Area Location | Key Drop Item | Drop Chance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Todo** | Culling Games Raid | Vibra Slap | **1%** |
| **JudgeMan** | Culling Games Spawn | Gavel Hammer | **1%** |
| **Yuji Shinjuku** | Next to Heian Sukuna | Chosen Vessel | **2%** |
| **Kashimo** | Shinjuku Stone Platform | Electric Pole | **1%** |
