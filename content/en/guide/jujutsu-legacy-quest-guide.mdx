<Section icon={Target} title="Jujutsu Legacy Quest Guide: Level 1 to 500">

The first 500 levels are the most critical for setting up your character's foundation. During this phase, your primary goal is to accumulate enough experience to unlock the Shibuya Metro area. Following a structured **jujutsu legacy quest guide** ensures you don't waste time on low-yield enemies. Focus heavily on **Strength** or **Technique** stats depending on your preferred combat style to ensure you can one-shot basic mobs.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Level 5 Sprint">
    Immediately head to the first quest NPC at Jujutsu High. Defeat the starter curses until you hit Level 5. Once reached, open the menu and input all active codes to secure at least 50+ Technique Spins.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Shibuya Approach (Level 125+)">
    Move toward the Shibuya bridge area. Here, you will find the Takuma Ino quest line. This is a repeatable loop that offers high EXP for the time invested. Stay here until your damage is high enough to tackle the Graveyard.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Graveyard Grind (Level 275+)">
    The Graveyard quests involving the Eso route are essential. The enemies here are slightly tougher but drop better currency. Use area-of-effect (AoE) skills to clear groups of three or more to maximize your leveling speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Metro Entry (Level 300-450)">
    Enter the Shibuya Metro. This is where the game transitions from questing to boss farming. You will encounter Choso and Mahito. These bosses are checkpoints that test your build's durability and burst damage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Stat Allocation Warning">
Avoid a "balanced" build early on. Spreading points too thinly across Health, Strength, and Technique will make boss fights significantly harder. Focus on one offensive stat until it reaches at least 500 points.
</Callout>

</Section>
