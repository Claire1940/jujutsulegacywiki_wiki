Starting out in a cursed world can feel overwhelming due to the multiple layers of progression thrown at you simultaneously. Following a structured leveling path prevents wasted time and keeps your character's power level on par with the enemies you face.

  <Step num="1" title="Enter the Experience and Locate Quests">
    Launch the game and locate the starter quest NPCs near the spawn area. These early missions provide rapid experience points and help you learn the basic combat mechanics, such as basic attacks, blocking, and dodging.

  <Step num="2" title="Reach Level 5 via Basic Grinding">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Cursed Techniques Tier List">

Cursed techniques dictate your active skill kit, range, and combat style. While some techniques excel at clearing large waves of minor enemies, others are designed to burst down high-health bosses or lock down opponents in PvP duels.

| Tier | Cursed Techniques | Primary Role | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S** | Heian Shrine, Ten Shadows V2, Limitless, Rika | High-End Burst & Control | Boss Raids, Endgame PvP, Fast Farming |
| **A** | Cursed Manipulation, Mythical Beast Amber V2, Idle Transfiguration, Sky Manipulation, Projection, Disaster Flames | Area Pressure & Mobility | Mid-to-Late Grinding, Elite Bosses |
| **B** | Ice Formation, Disaster Tides, Ten Shadows, Puppet Manipulation, Cursed Speech, Anti Gravity, Moon Dregs | Crowdflow & Setup | Solo Grinding, Mid-Game Progression |
| **C** | Divergent Fist, Missile Fists, Death Sentence, Ratio, Comedian, Boogie Woogie | Starter Melee & Niche Utility | Early Levels, Experimental Builds |

### Tier Breakdowns

- **S-Tier**: These techniques offer massive damage multipliers, iframe skills, or powerful summon options (like Rika). They are highly versatile and remain effective up to the level 2000 cap.
- **A-Tier**: These are excellent keeps that can comfortably carry you through any content. Techniques like Disaster Flames offer incredible AoE damage, making them perfect for clustered quest mobs.
- **B-Tier**: Usable and reliable. Ten Shadows (Base) and Ice Formation provide decent crowd control and steady damage but lack the raw scaling required for fast late-game raid clears.
- **C-Tier**: These are starter techniques. If you roll one of these, use it to complete early quests, but prepare to spend your next batch of spins to replace them as soon as possible.

<Callout type="warning" title="Spin Management">
Never roll away a high-tier technique unless you have a backup stockpile of at least 50–100 spins. Rerolling blindly can leave you stranded with a weak C-tier technique and no way to farm efficiently.
</Callout>

Your character's build is divided into three distinct layers: Cursed Techniques, Clans, and Races. Each system requires a specific spin type to reroll, and managing these resources strategically is the key to creating a cohesive build.

| Spin Type | System Affected | Progression Strategy | Mistakes to Avoid |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technique Spins** | Active Skill Kit | Prioritize first; defines your main damage type and range. | Rerolling a good farming technique too early. |
| **Clan Spins** | Passive Stat Boosts | Roll after stabilizing your technique to boost specific stats. | Treating clan bonuses as early-game priorities. |
| **Race Spins** | Base Stat Scaling | Save for the endgame to fine-tune your final stat distribution. | Replacing a decent race before your build is set. |

### Build Customization Strategy

Your cursed technique dictates how you fight, while your clan and race act as passive multipliers that enhance those abilities. For example, if you roll a technique that scales heavily with cursed energy, you should eventually aim for a clan and race that offer maximum energy pools and cooldown reduction.

<Callout type="tip" title="Reroll Order">
Always lock in your cursed technique first. Once you have an S-tier or A-tier technique that you plan to keep permanently, begin spending your Clan and Race spins to match its scaling requirements.
</Callout>
