<Section icon={Map} title="Core Mechanics of Fighting Styles">

Fighting styles serve as the foundation of close-quarters combat in Jujutsu Legacy. Unlike cursed techniques that rely on cursed energy, fighting styles derive their power directly from your physical attributes. To build a powerful character, understanding how these styles function, scale, and interact with other equipment is essential.

Every player starts with the default Normal style. To upgrade to more advanced fighting styles, you must defeat specific bosses throughout the world to obtain rare drop items. Using one of these items will immediately equip the associated style. 

However, inventory management is critical when dealing with these combat systems. You can only have one fighting style equipped at any given time. If you choose to replace your current style with a new one, the previous style is overwritten. To regain a lost style, you must obtain and use its associated drop item again.

<Callout type="warning" title="Style Overwrite Warning">
Using a new fighting style item will permanently overwrite your current style. Always ensure you have a spare drop item or are fully committed to the change before consuming a new style item.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="In-Depth Analysis of Every Fighting Style">

Each fighting style offers a unique moveset, combo potential, and synergy with specific builds. Choosing the right style depends on your progression stage and preferred playstyle.

### Normal Style
The default starting style. It features basic punch and kick combos with low scaling. While it is sufficient for clearing early-game tutorial quests, you should aim to replace it as soon as you reach the Culling Games.

### The High Roller
This style requires the Gambler Orb, which drops from the Gambler boss at a highly elusive 0.1% rate. It is a high-risk, high-reward style that focuses on unpredictable strike patterns and critical hit modifiers. It is favored by end-game players who enjoy RNG-based burst damage.

### Strongest Style
Dropped by Gojo V2 with a generous 10% drop rate, the Strongest Style is the most accessible high-tier style in the game. It mimics Gojo's physical dominance, offering fast, heavy-hitting strikes and excellent crowd control. This is the recommended style for mid-game progression and efficient boss farming.

### Imperial Curse Arts
Obtained from the Divine General Raid at a 2% drop rate, this style focuses on tactical spacing and defensive counters. It allows players to control the pace of the fight, making it highly effective for challenging raid content and group encounters.

### Raijin Strike
Dropped by Kashimo in Shinjuku at a 0.5% rate, this style infuses your physical attacks with electrical energy. It features rapid movement speed, lightning-fast combos, and minor stun mechanics, making it perfect for high-mobility playstyles.

### Cursed Vessel
Requiring the Itadori Finger (0.3% drop from Yuji 120% in Shinjuku), the Cursed Vessel style utilizes aggressive, close-range physical combos. It features high base scaling and delayed impact damage, making it a premier choice for pure Strength builds.
